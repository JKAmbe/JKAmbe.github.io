ABOUT ME

Who Am I?
My name is JKAmbe! Ok, that's not my real name, my real name is Jansen Chandra, I'm a recent graduate from University of Technology Sydney as a Bachelor in Information Technology, majoring in Interaction design, specializing in Game Design and software.
What can I do?
I can do a lot! Really! I'm a software developer first, so I'm comfortable with anything from C++, Java, phyton, and C#. Game engine wise I've experimented with most of the easily available ones like Unity, Unreal, godot. I even dabble with stuff like vr, javascript, webXR! Other than coding, as a student in Interaction design, I'm quite versed in UX and UI design, and as an (aspiring) game developer, I can do 3d modelling, sounds, and animations!
How do I contact you?
The best way to contact me is via discord or emails, I respond especially quickly with discord, otherwise except a response within 24 hours.

MY WORK

Nightmare Slayers
My most recent work! It was a collaboration between me and 3 other game design students during my last semester at UTS and was showcased during the 2023 UTS tech fest. The pitch was to made a starfox 64 style of 2 axis shoot'em'up' but with multiple playable character and our own twist of style.

I played multiple role during the game's production. I managed tickets, programmed a LOT, and I even get to draw and model my own characters. A lot happened as always, getting burned out, personal issues, technical problems but we got there in the end and we're all VERY proud with how it turned out.
Newtype Dodgeball
The pitch for this one is to make gundam style funnels in Unreal Engine and turn it into a multiplayer game. Bear in mind that this is the first time me and my teammate touched Unreal engine. This one is yet another collaborative work, this time with only one other student with me. We're allowed to do pretty much anything, both of us love gundams, and well we decided to go a bit overboard.

The result speaks for itself, REALLY proud of this one! It's not available for play at the moment, since we haven't figured out the logistic of deploying it. But I got a playlist (12 videos) here for the entire process, and a github repo to clone to be played in unreal editor
That time I got reincarnated as a botanist and forgot to study for my botanical assignment (x7)
This one got showcased for the 2022 UTS student games showcase. It was a collaboration between me and 5 other game design students. For this project, we have to work around a no violent restriction and follow a randomized theme. The pitch that we end up with was a point and click style puzzle game set in a magical world where you work as the botanist.

We each got to do our own level, mines are the final and the tutorial. I got to do a LOT of the programming, and modelling for this one. I was the most experienced member in the group, and I think it went quite well for my first real game
